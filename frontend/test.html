About Us

Bradley High School, a Salvation Army Church mixed boarding school located in Bindura, Mashonaland Central Province, Zimbabwe, has a rich history dating back to its founding in 1926 by Major James and Captain Bennett. Originally known as Bradley Institute, it began as a boys' school and has since evolved into a co-educational institution offering a comprehensive secondary education. The school has overcome historical challenges, including periods of closure due to water shortages and the war, to re-emerge as a leading educational institution. Bradley High School is committed to providing a nurturing environment that fosters academic, spiritual, and personal growth for all its students.

As a mission school, religious values are an integral part of daily life, with students encouraged to participate in church services and religious lessons. The school also emphasizes practical skills, with historical and ongoing programs in areas like carpentry and agriculture, complementing its strong academic curriculum.

Academics

Curriculum

Bradley High School follows the Zimbabwe School Examinations Council (ZIMSEC) curriculum, preparing students for national O-Level and A-Level examinations. The school has consistently achieved high pass rates, ranking among the top schools in the country.

Exam Performance

In recent years, Bradley High School has recorded impressive pass rates, with O-Level pass rates often exceeding 85% and A-Level pass rates reaching over 90%. For instance, in 2024, the school achieved a 96.4% pass rate at O' Level and a 100% pass rate at A' Level. The school was ranked 20th out of the top 100 ordinary-level schools in Zimbabwe for 2023, achieving an 86.5% pass rate.

Holistic Development

Beyond academics, the school emphasizes holistic development, offering a range of extra-curricular activities and practical training programs in areas such as agriculture, carpentry, and animal husbandry.

Admissions

For Form One intake, enrollment is typically managed through the Ministry of Education's EMAP platform after the Grade 7 results are published. While the cut-off for 2025 Form 1 intake was set at 9 units, parents are advised that submitting a child's baptism certificate, report, and an application letter before the exams may increase enrollment chances.

The school may give preference to students who are Salvationists, though it also accepts students from outside the Salvation Army.

While specific application forms for 2025 Form One places are not available, prospective students and parents are encouraged to contact the school directly for the most up-to-date admissions information and to arrange a campus tour.
